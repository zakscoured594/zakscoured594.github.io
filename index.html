NAS Storage Explained

A NAS (Network Attached Storage) is a dedicated storage system connected to your network. It allows computers, phones and media devices to access files from one central location.

🏠 Home NAS

Capacity:
4TB - 40TB

Uses:
Photos, movies, backups, personal cloud

⚙️ Advanced NAS

Capacity:
40TB - 200TB+

Uses:
Media servers and small businesses

🏢 Enterprise NAS

Capacity:
Hundreds of TB to Petabytes

Uses:
Companies and research facilities

RAID Protection Types

RAID Description
RAID 0 Maximum speed but no protection
RAID 1 Data mirrored on multiple drives
RAID 5 Balance between storage and protection
RAID 6 Extra protection for large systems
